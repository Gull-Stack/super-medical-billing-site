Privacy Policy

How we protect your information and PHI.

Effective Date: February 17, 2026  |  Last Updated: February 17, 2026

Super Medical Billing ("we," "us," "our"), a product of GullStack Trust, is committed to protecting the privacy and security of all information entrusted to us, including Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH).

1. Information We Collect

Protected Health Information (PHI)

In providing medical billing services, we may process the following PHI on behalf of covered entities:

  • Patient names, dates of birth, addresses, and contact information
  • Social Security Numbers (SSNs) and insurance member IDs
  • Insurance and payer information
  • Diagnosis codes (ICD-10), procedure codes (CPT/HCPCS), and treatment information
  • Claims data, remittance advice, and payment information
  • Eligibility and authorization information
  • Provider National Provider Identifiers (NPIs)

Account Information

  • Practice name, address, and contact information
  • User names, email addresses, and authentication credentials
  • Billing and payment information for our services

Technical Information

  • IP addresses, browser type, and device information
  • Audit logs of system access and actions
  • Session data and usage analytics

2. How We Use Information

We use PHI strictly as permitted under HIPAA and as directed by our covered entity clients for:

  • Submitting claims to payers and clearinghouses
  • Verifying patient eligibility and benefits
  • Processing electronic remittance advice (ERA/835)
  • Managing denials and appeals
  • Generating reports for practice management
  • Credentialing and provider enrollment

We do not sell, rent, or share PHI for marketing purposes. Ever.

3. How We Protect Information

Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption at Rest: AES-256-GCM encryption for all PHI stored in our databases
  • Encryption in Transit: TLS 1.3 for all data transmitted between clients and our servers
  • Access Control: Role-based access control (RBAC) with JWT authentication
  • Audit Logging: Complete audit trail on every data access and modification
  • Session Management: Automatic session timeouts and secure token handling

Administrative Safeguards

  • Designated Security Officer responsible for HIPAA compliance
  • Regular workforce training on privacy and security policies
  • Background checks on all personnel with PHI access
  • Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all subcontractors
  • Regular risk assessments and security audits

Physical Safeguards

  • SOC 2 compliant hosting infrastructure (Railway)
  • Data centers with physical access controls and monitoring
  • Redundant systems and regular backups

4. Data Retention

We retain PHI for the minimum period necessary to fulfill our obligations:

  • Active accounts: PHI retained for the duration of the service agreement
  • Post-termination: PHI retained for 7 years following termination, as required by federal and state regulations
  • Audit logs: Retained for a minimum of 6 years per HIPAA requirements
  • Upon request: PHI returned or destroyed per the terms of the BAA

5. Patient Rights

While we act as a Business Associate and do not have a direct relationship with patients, we support covered entities in fulfilling patient rights under HIPAA, including:

  • Right to Access: Patients may request access to their PHI through their healthcare provider
  • Right to Amendment: Patients may request corrections to their PHI
  • Right to Accounting: Patients may request an accounting of disclosures of their PHI
  • Right to Restriction: Patients may request restrictions on certain uses of their PHI
  • Right to Confidential Communications: Patients may request alternative communication methods

We will cooperate fully with covered entities to respond to patient rights requests within the timeframes required by HIPAA.

6. Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will:

  • Notify the affected covered entity within 24 hours of discovering the breach
  • Provide the identity of each individual whose PHI was or is believed to have been affected
  • Provide a description of the type of information involved
  • Provide a description of what we are doing to investigate, mitigate, and prevent future breaches
  • Cooperate fully with the covered entity's breach notification obligations to individuals and the HHS

7. Subcontractors and Third Parties

We may share PHI with the following categories of subcontractors, all of whom are bound by BAAs:

  • Clearinghouses: Claim.MD for EDI claim submission
  • Eligibility Services: Availity for real-time eligibility verification
  • Hosting Provider: Railway for SOC 2 compliant infrastructure

We do not disclose PHI to any third party without a BAA in place or as otherwise permitted by HIPAA.

8. Cookies and Analytics

Our marketing website may use cookies for analytics purposes. No PHI is collected through cookies. The application platform uses only essential session cookies required for authentication and security.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated to our covered entity clients at least 30 days in advance. The "Last Updated" date above reflects the most recent revision.
