BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENT

This Business Associate Agreement ("BAA" or "Agreement") is entered into by and between the healthcare provider or practice ("Covered Entity") and GullStack Trust, doing business as Super Medical Billing ("Business Associate"), effective as of the date of the last signature below (the "Effective Date").

1. Definitions

Terms used but not otherwise defined in this Agreement shall have the same meaning as those terms in the HIPAA Rules (45 CFR Parts 160 and 164).

  • "PHI" means Protected Health Information as defined in 45 CFR ¬ß 160.103, limited to the information created, received, maintained, or transmitted by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity.
  • "Electronic PHI" or "ePHI" means PHI that is transmitted or maintained in electronic media.
  • "HIPAA Rules" means the Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules at 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164.
  • "Services" means the medical billing, claims processing, eligibility verification, remittance processing, and related services provided by Business Associate.

2. Obligations of Business Associate

Business Associate agrees to:

  1. Use and Disclosure Limitations: Not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this Agreement or as required by law.
  2. Safeguards: Use appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent unauthorized use or disclosure of PHI, including:
    • AES-256-GCM encryption for all ePHI at rest
    • TLS 1.3 encryption for all ePHI in transit
    • Role-based access controls with multi-factor authentication
    • Regular security risk assessments
    • Workforce training on HIPAA requirements
  3. Reporting: Report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure of PHI not provided for by this Agreement, including any Security Incident or Breach of Unsecured PHI, within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery.
  4. Subcontractors: Ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of the Business Associate agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to the Business Associate with respect to such information.
  5. Access to PHI: Make available PHI in a Designated Record Set to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an Individual, within fifteen (15) business days of request.
  6. Amendment of PHI: Make any amendment(s) to PHI in a Designated Record Set as directed by Covered Entity within fifteen (15) business days.
  7. Accounting of Disclosures: Maintain and make available information required to provide an accounting of disclosures as required by 45 CFR ¬ß 164.528.
  8. HHS Access: Make internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of Health and Human Services for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with the HIPAA Rules.
  9. Minimum Necessary: Limit use, disclosure, and requests of PHI to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

3. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI as necessary to:

  1. Perform the Services described in the underlying service agreement
  2. Carry out its legal responsibilities
  3. Provide Data Aggregation services relating to the Health Care Operations of the Covered Entity
  4. Report violations of law to appropriate federal and state authorities
  5. Perform management and administration activities, provided that disclosures are required by law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient

4. Obligations of Covered Entity

Covered Entity agrees to:

  1. Notify Business Associate of any limitations in its Notice of Privacy Practices that may affect Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI
  2. Notify Business Associate of any changes in, or revocation of, the permission by an Individual to use or disclose their PHI
  3. Notify Business Associate of any restriction on the use or disclosure of PHI that Covered Entity has agreed to in accordance with 45 CFR ¬ß 164.522
  4. Obtain any necessary consents or authorizations required under applicable law prior to furnishing PHI to Business Associate

5. Term and Termination

  1. Term: This Agreement shall be effective as of the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until the underlying service agreement is terminated or all PHI is destroyed or returned.
  2. Termination for Cause: Either party may terminate this Agreement if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure the breach within thirty (30) days of written notice.
  3. Effect of Termination: Upon termination, Business Associate shall return or destroy all PHI received from or created on behalf of Covered Entity, if feasible. If return or destruction is not feasible, protections under this Agreement shall extend to such PHI and further uses and disclosures shall be limited to the purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible.
  4. Survival: The obligations of Business Associate under Sections 2 and 5(c) shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

6. Security Incident Procedures

  1. Business Associate shall implement and maintain a security incident response plan
  2. Business Associate shall identify and respond to suspected or known Security Incidents and mitigate harmful effects
  3. Business Associate shall document Security Incidents and their outcomes
  4. Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery of a Security Incident involving PHI

7. Indemnification

Business Associate shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Covered Entity from and against any claims, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorney's fees) arising from or in connection with any breach of this Agreement by Business Associate or any of its subcontractors.

8. Miscellaneous

  1. Regulatory References: Any reference to a section of HIPAA Rules means the section as in effect or as amended.
  2. Amendment: This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both parties.
  3. No Third-Party Beneficiaries: Nothing in this Agreement shall confer any rights on any third party.
  4. Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by federal law, and to the extent not preempted, by the laws of the State of Utah.
  5. Entire Agreement: This Agreement, together with the underlying service agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter.
